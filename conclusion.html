Conclusion

Bringing it all together — key takeaways and a closing statement.

Key Takeaways

01

The 1987 Philippine Constitution guarantees freedom of expression, including in digital spaces.

02

RA 10175 criminalizes online offenses including cyber libel, identity theft, and hacking.

03

The law must distinguish between protected criticism of public figures and malicious attacks on private individuals.

04

Digital rights come with digital responsibilities — every post carries legal and moral weight.

05

A healthy democracy requires both free speech and protection from digital abuse.

"The internet is the 21st century's public. We must strive to keep it free in the Philippines—free from abuse, free from censorship, and free from fear. The law does not stop people from speaking; misusing the law does. As digital citizens, the best way to protect ourselves is not to stay quiet, but to speak up in an informed, responsible, and brave way."

— Jennyvieve Mahinay
