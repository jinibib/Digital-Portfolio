Digital Advocacy & Memes

Think Before You Click — Know your rights, know your limits.

Think Before You Click

Every post, comment, and share carries legal and moral weight.

Rights

Your Rights Online

  • Express opinions on public matters
  • Criticize government policies and officials
  • Share news and information
  • Engage in satire and commentary
  • Report wrongdoing in good faith
Limits

Your Limitations

  • Posting false facts about a private person
  • Threatening or harassing individuals
  • Sharing intimate images without consent
  • Impersonating someone online
  • Inciting violence or hatred
Violations

Examples of Violations

  • Creating fake accounts to defame someone
  • Spreading unverified rumors as facts
  • Doxxing (publishing private information)
  • Cyberbullying minors
  • Hacking into private accounts
Penalties

Legal Consequences

  • Cyber libel: up to 12 years imprisonment
  • Identity theft: 6–12 years + fines
  • Cybersex: 6–12 years + P200K fine
  • Hacking: 6 years + fines
  • Civil damages for emotional distress
Tips

Responsible Behavior Tips

  • Verify before you share — check sources
  • Distinguish opinion from fact in posts
  • Respect others' privacy and dignity
  • Report harmful content, don't amplify it
  • Seek legal advice before posting sensitive claims

Memes

Sometimes the best way to understand the law is through humor — but even memes have limits.
